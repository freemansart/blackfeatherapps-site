FAQ

What formats are supported?

Models: OBJ and FBX. Sources: NDI, MOV video files and PNG/JPG images.

What macOS version is required?

UVScope requires macOS 13.5 or later (Apple Silicon or Intel).

Does it work offline?

Yes. File-based preview works fully offline. NDI requires a network, licensing does not.

How does licensing work?

The trial runs for 10 days. After that, UVScope is activated using a license key tied to a hardware fingerprint.

What if I change my computer?

If your hardware fingerprint changes, a re-issue key is available.

How do I use NDI?

Select NDI as the source, choose the feed from the list, and UVScope applies it as a live texture on the model in realtime.

Can UVScope import cameras from FBX?

Yes. UVScope imports FBX cameras so you can review the scene through director or render camera angles.

What does camera lock mean?

Camera lock fixes the camera position while still allowing you to look around by rotating the view — ideal for consistent viewpoints during reviews.

Can I control face visibility and inactive sides?

Yes. UVScope supports Both / Front / Back face modes and inactive side display options such as Gray or Hide.

Is there scrubbing and looping for video files?

Yes. Video playback includes scrubbing, looping and play/pause controls for precise offline preview.

How does reference recording work?

UVScope records exactly what you see to H.264 + AAC, including video audio and microphone voice.

Can I balance microphone and video audio levels?

Yes. Separate gain controls and level meters help keep voice comments clear over the content audio.

Is UVScope suitable for client presentations?

Yes. The clean UI, shortcuts and quick view switching make it ideal for art directors and client reviews.
